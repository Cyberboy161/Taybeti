🛡️ Why the custom keyboard matters

Every other app uses your phone's system keyboard. That keyboard can — and often does — record what you type and send it to Google, Apple, or a third‑party. Taybeti's built‑in keyboard completely isolates your typing from the system. No keylogger can touch it. No predictive text leaks your passphrase. It's the keyboard equivalent of a Faraday cage.

🔒 Per‑note encryption

Most apps encrypt your data with one master key — if that key is stolen, everything is exposed. Taybeti encrypts every single note with its own independent AES‑256‑GCM key derived from the passphrase you set. Cracking one note gives an attacker nothing on the others. Each note is its own fortress.

📵 Offline by design

Taybeti has no internet permission at all. It cannot connect to the internet. It cannot sync, cannot upload, cannot phone home. Your data literally cannot leave your device — by architecture, not by policy. Most apps say they're private; we made it physically impossible to be otherwise.

🧪 Self‑verifying login

No password hashes are stored — there's nothing to crack. When you log in, the app tries to decrypt a known string with your passphrase. If it succeeds, you're in. If it fails, the vault stays closed. An attacker with access to the raw data gets nothing but random‑looking encrypted bytes.
